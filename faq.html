🧬 Products & Compounds
Details on retatrutide, tirzepatide, NAD+, BPC-157 and all Alluvi compounds.
Retatrutide (LY3437943) is a next-generation peptide that acts as a triple agonist of GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors simultaneously. Unlike single-pathway GLP-1 agonists such as semaglutide (Ozempic), retatrutide's triple-receptor mechanism delivers significantly superior metabolic modulation in research settings. Phase 2 clinical trials published in the New England Journal of Medicine showed an average of 22.5% body weight reduction at 48 weeks — more than any other GLP-1 class compound tested to date. All Alluvi retatrutide is supplied for research and development purposes only.
  • Retatrutide: Triple agonist (GLP-1 + GIP + Glucagon) — 22.5% average weight reduction in Phase 2 trials
  • Semaglutide: Single GLP-1 agonist — ~15% weight reduction in STEP trials
Retatrutide's tri-agonist mechanism makes it particularly valuable for research investigating multi-pathway metabolic modulation, energy expenditure, and synergistic receptor interactions. Semaglutide is better suited for focused GLP-1 single-pathway mechanism studies. For research purposes, retatrutide represents the next generation beyond both semaglutide and tirzepatide.
Tirzepatide (the active molecule in Mounjaro) is a dual agonist targeting GLP-1 and GIP receptors — showing ~22% weight reduction. Retatrutide adds a third mechanism — glucagon receptor agonism — which increases resting energy expenditure significantly. In head-to-head research comparisons, retatrutide demonstrates superior fat oxidation and metabolic modulation due to this additional pathway. Both compounds are available as research-grade peptides from Alluvi Healthcare.
