The Alluvi Retatrutide pre-filled research pen is designed for ease of use in controlled laboratory settings. This step-by-step guide covers everything from preparation to post-use storage for both the 20mg and 40mg formats.

Before Use — Storage Check

  • Verify the pen has been stored at 2–8°C — do not use if frozen or exposed to heat above 25°C
  • Remove from refrigerator 15–30 minutes before use to allow the solution to reach room temperature
  • Inspect the solution — it should be clear and colourless. Do not use if cloudy, discoloured or contains particles
  • Check the seal is intact — do not use if the pen appears tampered with

Pen Setup and Priming

First Use Only — Priming

  1. Remove the pen cap
  2. Attach a new sterile needle following the needle manufacturer's instructions
  3. Dial the dose selector to the priming dose (typically 2 units or as specified in your protocol)
  4. Point the pen upwards, tap gently to move air bubbles to the top
  5. Press the dose button until a small drop appears at the needle tip — pen is primed and ready

Injection Procedure (Research Protocol Context)

  1. Select appropriate subcutaneous tissue site per your research protocol
  2. Clean the site with an alcohol swab and allow to dry
  3. Pinch a fold of tissue gently between thumb and forefinger
  4. Insert the needle at a 45–90° angle depending on tissue depth
  5. Press the dose button firmly and hold for 10 seconds before withdrawing
  6. Release the tissue fold and withdraw the needle in one smooth motion
  7. Apply gentle pressure — do not rub the site

Site Rotation

For extended research protocols, rotate injection sites to avoid local tissue reactions. Common rotation patterns include alternating between different areas of the abdomen, or using multiple defined zones and moving systematically between them across successive doses.

After Use — Storage and Safety

  • Replace the needle cap immediately after use and dispose of the needle in a sharps container
  • Replace the pen cap and return to refrigerator at 2–8°C
  • Do not freeze — freezing permanently degrades the peptide
  • Used pens should be stored upright in refrigerator between doses
  • Record dose, date, time and site in your research log
